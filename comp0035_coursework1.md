#### What is Scrum?
> Scrum is a lightweight framework that helps people, teams and organizations generate value through adaptive solutions for complex problems.
> In a nutshell, Scrum requires a Scrum Master to foster an environment where:
> 1. A Product Owner orders the work for a complex problem into a Product Backlog.
> 2. The Scrum Team turns a selection of the work into an Increment of value during a Sprint.
> 3. The Scrum Team and its stakeholders inspect the results and adjust for the next Sprint.
> 4. Repeat
> (The 2020 scrum GUIDE 2020)

#### What is CRISP-DM?
> CRISP-DM (CRoss-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) is a model for data science cyclical process in 6 phases (Business Understanding, Data Understanding, Data Preparation, Modeling, Evaluation and Deployment). In addition, the entire process can be repeated for iterative deployments. (Abdollazadeh, 2021)

#### How to combine them?
Each stage of Scrum can be integrated with CRISP-DM. An analytics project may be broken down into numerous Scrum sprints, each of which uses all of the CRISP-DM stages. As an example, the first will spend more time on upfront research, such as business needs, amount of data, current practices and issues. Making a basic model with only three inputs, for example, may be the next step. After that, one or two sprints can be dedicated to determining the appropriate model specification. The deployment structure may be fine-tuned and hardened in the final sprint. Each sprint covers every stage of CRISP-DM once more, although the main focus switches. In Scrum, each Sprint and corresponding output is time-limited. Communication and review with stakeholders and teams is required for every sprint. Each builds on the previous's results and lessons learned, resulting in a final product that the project stakeholders understand, approve, and own.

### Selection criteria and justification of selection
#### Criteria
The selection of the methodology is based on some criteria.
1. **Team background**: If team members have relevant experience about the methodology or solving revelant problems, this will greatly improve the efficiency and proficiency of the approach and reduce upfront learning and communication costs.

2. **Speed of completion**: The ability to quickly meet commercial or stakeholder needs, seize market opportunities and capture market share.

3. **Cost**: The need to reduce waste in the budget while ensuring completion of the project.

> Companies pay a premium of as much as 60% on time and budget when they use poor requirements practices on their projects. (Hodgson, 2020)

4. **Communication**: Reduce time and communication costs through efficient team communication in collaboration

> Poorly defined applications (miscommunication between business and IT) contribute to a 66% project failure rate, costing U.S. businesses at least $30 billion every year. (Hodgson, 2020)

5. **Deliverables**: The type and use of final deliverables

## Definition of the business need
### Problem definition
Volcanoes are difficult to compare with other natural disasters such as floods. Maintaining insurance and infrastructure investment amidst a range of hazards is challenging. Governments, companies and communities need to consider these risk prevention measures to ensure continued operations and future prosperity. Robust statistical models may be developed for often recurring hazard events, such as floods, using smaller events with high-frequency to represent "scaled-up" more uncommon cycle. General public is accustomed with ideas like "100-year flood" and "one-in-50-year occurrence," but is this method practical when it comes to volcanic eruptions? Volcanic hazard appraisal has frequently sought probabilistic estimates, but the lack of sufficiently long-period eruption data sets has hindered the development of predictive models. (Turner et al., 2007) Therefore, in this project, some dimensions such as the number of eruptions, the duration of the eruption and the VEI are presented by visualising the eruption data. It is hoped that this will support some basic data presentation for model development, improve the accuracy of predictions and reduce losses to society and humans.

> VEI: Volcano Eruption Index, a measure of explosive magnitude for historical volcanism (Newhall & Self, 1982)
